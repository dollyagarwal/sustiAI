Executive Summary

This report analyzes BlueSky Corp's event-related emissions per attendee across various categories and GHG scopes. The Sustainability Summit emerges as the most carbon-intensive event on a per-attendee basis, with digital and accommodation categories being significant contributors.

Key Insights

  • The "Sustainability Summit" event recorded the highest total emissions per attendee (939.89 kg CO₂e), significantly higher than "Quarterly Business Review" (484.61 kg CO₂e) and "Product Launch" (83.40 kg CO₂e).
  • Across all events, Accommodation (304.67 kg CO₂e), Catering (280.07 kg CO₂e), and Digital (231.49 kg CO₂e) are the top three emission categories per attendee.
  • GHG Scope 1 accounts for the largest share of per-attendee emissions (529.78 kg CO₂e), closely followed by Scope 2 (502.25 kg CO₂e) and Scope 3 (475.87 kg CO₂e), indicating a relatively balanced distribution across scopes, though Scope 1 is slightly dominant.
  • The highest single emission category per attendee was 172.08 kg CO₂e for Digital emissions under Scope 1 during the Sustainability Summit, highlighting specific hotspots.

Recommendations

  1. Optimize High-Impact Events: Focus on reducing emissions for events like the "Sustainability Summit" by scrutinizing high-emitting categories such as Accommodation and Digital.
  2. Strategic Category Reduction: Implement initiatives to reduce emissions from Accommodation (e.g., choosing greener venues, promoting local attendance) and Catering (e.g., plant-based menus, waste reduction programs) across all events.
  3. Scope-Specific Interventions: Develop targeted strategies for Scope 1 emissions, such as optimizing onsite energy consumption and waste management, while also addressing Scope 2 and Scope 3 through renewable energy sourcing and sustainable supply chain engagement.
